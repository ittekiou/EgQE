# **Social ψ — Persistence and Maintenance**
## — The Externalization of Time —

---

## **Abstract**

This section defines Social ψ as the structure through which time, generated by folding, is externally maintained.

Unlike biological ψ, which persists internally, Social ψ requires active maintenance through repeated inscription, reference, and renewal.

When maintenance ceases, Social ψ does not collapse into matter.  
Instead, it decays into residual traces.

Thus, Social ψ introduces a new asymmetry within the modes of encounter: persistence versus maintenance.

---

## **0. Introduction — From Generation to Maintenance**

In HEG-17, time was shown to emerge through folding and persistence (ψ).

However, in human society, time appears as something external and objective.

This section addresses the shift:

**from internally generated time  
to externally maintained time.**

---

## **1. Definition of Social ψ**

**Definition:**

**Social ψ is the structure in which time, generated through folding, is externalized and maintained as shared symbols.**

This includes:

- calendars
    
- anniversaries
    
- historical records
    
- timestamps
    

These are not time itself.

👉 **They are maintained inscriptions of time.**

---

## **2. Persistence vs Maintenance**

A fundamental asymmetry emerges:

- biological ψ → persistence
    
- social ψ → maintenance
    

Thus:

**Life persists.  
Society is maintained.**

---

## **3. The Necessity of Maintenance**

Social ψ does not sustain itself.

It requires:

- repetition
    
- recognition
    
- updating
    
- participation
    

Without these:

👉 **it ceases to function as time**

---

## **4. Failure Modes**

Failure differs between life and society:

- life → collapse (loss of folding)
    
- society → oblivion (loss of maintenance)
    

Thus:

**collapse vs oblivion**

---

## **5. Residualization of Social ψ**

### — When Maintenance Ceases —

When Social ψ is no longer maintained:

- inscriptions remain
    
- symbols persist
    
- traces (ΔZ) are still present
    

However:

- no one refers to them
    
- no updates occur
    
- no shared recognition exists
    

Thus:

**Social ψ decays into residual traces.**

---

## **6. Residual Traces (ΔZ)**

Residual traces are:

- preserved but inactive
    
- existent but non-operative
    

Examples:

- unread monuments
    
- forgotten records
    
- abandoned archives
    

Thus:

**what remains is not time, but trace.**

---

## **7. Criterion of Social ψ**

We can now define a criterion:

**Social ψ exists only where maintenance is active.**

Thus:

- active → Social ψ
    
- fragile → weakening Social ψ
    
- residual → no Social ψ (only ΔZ)
    

---

## **8. Non-Equivalence with Death**

Unlike life:

- life → death → matter
    

Social ψ does not “die.”

Instead:

👉 **it is forgotten**

---

## **9. Repositioning Time**

We now state:

**Time is not merely generated.  
It must be maintained.**

---

## **Conclusion**

Social ψ is the maintenance of time.

Without maintenance:

- time does not persist
    
- only traces remain
    

Thus:

**Time is generated by persistence  
and stabilized by maintenance.**

# **社会ψ — 持続と維持**
## — 時間の外部化 —

---

## **概要**

本節は社会ψを、折り返しによって生成された時間が外部記号として維持される構造として定義する。

生命ψが内在的持続であるのに対し、社会ψは外在的維持を必要とする。

維持が停止すると、社会ψは消滅せず、残滓へと移行する。

---

## **0. 導入 — 生成から維持へ**

時間は折り返しによって生まれる。

しかし社会においては：

👉 **時間は維持される**

---

## **1. 定義**

**社会ψとは、折り返しによって生成された時間が、外部記号として維持される構造である。**

---

## **2. 非対称性**

- 生命ψ：持続
    
- 社会ψ：維持
    

👉 **Life persists. Society is maintained.**

---

## **3. 維持の必要性**

社会ψは：

- 更新
    
- 参照
    
- 共有
    

を必要とする

---

## **4. 失敗様式**

- 生命：崩壊
    
- 社会：忘却
    

👉 **collapse vs oblivion**

---

## **5. 残滓化**

維持が止まると：

- 痕跡（ΔZ）は残る
    
- しかし機能しない
    

👉 **残滓となる**

---

## **6. 残滓とは何か**

- 読まれない石碑
    
- 忘れられた記録
    

👉 存在するが作動しない

---

## **7. 判定基準**

社会ψは：

👉 維持されているときのみ存在する

---

## **8. 死との非対称**

社会ψは死なない。

👉 **忘れられる**

---

## **9. 時間の再定義**

**時間は生まれるだけでは足りない  
維持されなければならない**

---

## ■ 結語

**時間は持続によって生まれ  
維持によって社会化される**
