# **Embedded Folding**
## — Institutional and Cultural Maintenance —

---

## **Abstract**

This section develops the concept of embedded folding within Social ψ.

Folding, originally an internal operation, appears externally as traces (ΔZ) that are maintained within social structures.

These traces are sustained in two distinct modes:

- institutional maintenance (top-down, enforced)
    
- cultural maintenance (distributed, participatory)
    

Thus, time in society is not only maintained, but maintained differently.

---

## **0. Introduction — Folding in External Form**

In HEG-18-1, Social ψ was defined as the maintenance of externally inscribed time.

However, what is maintained is not folding itself.

👉 It is the trace of folding (ΔZ).

This section examines how these traces are embedded and sustained.

---

## **1. Embedded Folding**

**Definition:**

**Embedded folding is the condition in which traces generated by folding (ΔZ) are inscribed into external structures and maintained.**

Thus:

- folding → generation
    
- trace (ΔZ) → inscription
    
- maintenance → Social ψ
    

---

## **2. Two Modes of Maintenance**

Embedded folding appears in two distinct modes:

- institutional
    
- cultural
    

Both maintain time, but differently.

---

## **3. Institutional Maintenance**

Institutions maintain time through enforcement.

Examples:

- calendars
    
- legal systems
    
- standard time
    
- state-defined schedules
    

Characteristics:

- external authority
    
- rule-based
    
- centralized control
    

Thus:

**Institutions stabilize time.**

---

## **4. Cultural Maintenance**

Cultures maintain time through participation.

Examples:

- festivals
    
- rituals
    
- customs
    
- language practices
    

Characteristics:

- distributed agency
    
- repetition
    
- shared enactment
    

Thus:

**Cultures rhythmize time.**

---

## **5. Structural Difference**

The difference lies in maintenance:

- institution → exogenous (top-down)
    
- culture → distributed (participatory)
    

Thus:

**time is either imposed or enacted.**

---

## **6. Stability vs Plasticity**

Institutions:

- high stability
    
- low flexibility
    

Cultures:

- high flexibility
    
- low stability
    

Thus:

**two complementary dynamics of time.**

---

## **7. Failure Modes**

Maintenance fails differently:

- institution → cessation (decision-based)
    
- culture → decay (participation loss)
    

Thus:

**stopping vs fading**

---

## **8. Temporal Effects**

Institutional time:

- homogeneous
    
- standardized
    
- synchronized
    

Cultural time:

- rhythmic
    
- variable
    
- experiential
    

Thus:

**time splits into uniformity and rhythm.**

---

## **9. Repositioning Society**

Society is not a single structure.

It is:

👉 **a field of heterogeneous maintenance modes**

---

## **Conclusion**

Embedded folding sustains time through two distinct modes:

- stabilization (institution)
    
- rhythmization (culture)
    

Thus:

**Society maintains time by both fixing and re-enacting it.**

# **埋め込まれた折り返し**
## — 制度と文化の維持 —

---

## **概要**

本節は、折り返しによって生成された痕跡（ΔZ）が社会構造に埋め込まれ、維持される様式を論じる。

社会ψは単一ではなく：

- 制度的維持
    
- 文化的維持
    

という二様式を持つ。

---

## **0. 導入 — 外部化された折り返し**

社会にあるのは折り返しそのものではない。

👉 **折り返しの痕跡である**

それが外部に刻印され、維持される。

---

## **1. 埋め込まれた折り返し**

**定義：**  

**埋め込まれた折り返しとは、折り返しの痕跡（ΔZ）が外部構造に刻印され、維持される状態である。**

---

## **2. 二つの維持様式**

- 制度
    
- 文化
    

---

## **3. 制度的維持**

制度は：

- 規則
    
- 権力
    
- 標準
    

によって時間を維持する

👉 **時間を固定する**

---

## **4. 文化的維持**

文化は：

- 反復
    
- 参加
    
- 共有
    

によって時間を維持する

👉 **時間を生き直す**

---

## **5. 構造差**

- 制度：外在的
    
- 文化：分散的
    

---

## **6. 安定と可塑性**

- 制度：安定
    
- 文化：可塑
    

---

## **7. 失効様式**

- 制度：停止
    
- 文化：減衰
    

👉 **停止 vs 減衰**

---

## **8. 時間の分岐**

- 制度：均質時間
    
- 文化：リズム時間
    

---

## **9. 社会の再定義**

社会は：

👉 **複数の維持様式の場である**

---

## ■ 結語

**制度は時間を固定し  
文化は時間を生き直す**
