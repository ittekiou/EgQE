## 0. Introduction

The universe does not begin from nothing.

It begins from the failure of identity.

Perfect sameness never holds.  
At the moment of attempting to be identical, a minimal discrepancy emerges.

That discrepancy makes update possible.

---

## 1. floc — Pre-differential Non-Identity

In this work, _floc_ is defined as:

> **a state in which non-identity has not yet appeared as difference**

floc is characterized by:

- non-locality (no locality)
    
- atemporality (no temporal order)
    
- absence of scale (no metric structure)
    

floc is not a field.  
It is a **pre-field**, prior to any field-like structure.

---

## 2. lag — Residue of Failed Identification

Within floc, an operation toward identity emerges (a tendency toward “=”).

However, perfect identity cannot be achieved.

The minimal residue of this failure is:

> **lag**

---

### Trigger Condition (floc → lag)

```id="floc-lag-trigger"
floc + (tendency toward =) → residual ≠ → lag
```

lag is not accidental.

> **It arises necessarily as the failure of identity.**

---

## 3. Axioms of lag

1. **Non-zero condition**: ℓ > 0  
    No simultaneous update exists
    
2. **Locality**: ℓ = ℓ(x, t)  
    lag is distributed
    
3. **Lower bound**: ℓ ≥ ℓ_min  
    A minimal update unit exists
    
4. **Variability**: ℓ depends on the field  
    (gravity, medium, etc.)
    
5. **Directionality**: ℓ(i→j) ≠ ℓ(j→i) is allowed
    

---

## 4. Classification of lag

lag appears in different structural forms:

---

### 4.1 Symmetric lag

ℓ(i→j) = ℓ(j→i)

→ reversible updates  
→ no history

---

### 4.2 Asymmetric lag

ℓ(i→j) ≠ ℓ(j→i)

→ emergence of direction

> **The accumulation of asymmetric lag produces time-like phenomena**

---

### 4.3 Conserved lag

→ lag remains locally stored

> appears as mass and inertia

---

### 4.4 Divergent lag

→ lag spreads spatially

> appears as diffusion and cosmological expansion

---

Light does not “move” as an object.

> **What appears as light is the propagation limit of update.**
