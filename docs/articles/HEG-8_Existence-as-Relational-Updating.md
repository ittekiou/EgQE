### 1. The Question

What is existence?

Classical ontology has largely divided into two orientations:

- Explaining change from substance
    
- Explaining being from becoming
    

The former privileges stability.  
The latter privileges flux.

Both are insufficient.

Substance-based ontology treats updating as secondary.  
Process-based ontology struggles to explain persistence.

As long as this opposition remains, existence is forced to lean to one side.

---

### 2. The Thesis

The thesis of this paper is simple:

> Existence is relational updating under conservation.  
> Without presupposing updating, existence cannot be explained.

Updating here does not mean mere succession of events.  
It means redistribution of relations.

Conservation does not mean rigidity.  
It means that updating does not collapse the total relational structure.

Existence emerges only under the joint condition of updating and conservation.

---

### 3. Why Updating Is Necessary

Any ontology that does not presuppose updating inevitably falls into one of the following:

- Positing fixed entities
    
- Assuming a complete initial state
    
- Treating time as an external container
    

Yet irreversibility, boundary formation, identity, stability, collapse, error, and adaptation all require relational updating to be intelligible.

Existence is the trace of updating.

---

### 4. Why Conservation Is Necessary

Updating alone is not enough.

If updating were unrestricted, structure would not persist,  
boundaries would not form,  
stability would not arise.

Conservation is not reification.

It is:

> A topological constraint that prevents total relational breakdown.

Because conservation holds:

- updating can concentrate,
    
- concentration can freeze,
    
- freezing can generate boundaries.
    

Existence is a mode of updating that does not collapse.

---

### 5. Consequence

Existence is not substance.  
Existence is not stasis.  
Existence is not mere becoming.

Existence is:

> The sustained mode of relational updating under conservation constraints.

Remove updating, and existence freezes into fiction.  
Remove conservation, and existence dissolves into collapse.

---

### 6. One Line

> Existence without updating is illusion.  
> Updating without conservation is disintegration.
