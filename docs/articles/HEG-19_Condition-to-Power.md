## **Abstract**

This series develops a theory of support.

Support is not a force, nor a structure, nor an institution.  
It is the condition that binds encounter and differential trace, enabling persistence, maintenance, and distribution.

Through three sections, support is examined as:

- condition ([HEG-19-1](https://camp-us.net/articles/HEG-19-1_Support-Reconfigured.html))
    
- invisibility ([HEG-19-2](https://camp-us.net/articles/HEG-19-2_Invisibility-of-Support.html))
    
- asymmetry ([HEG-19-3](https://camp-us.net/articles/HEG-19-3_Distribution-of-Support.html))
