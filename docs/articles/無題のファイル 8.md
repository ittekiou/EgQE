# **HEG-19｜Distribution of Support**

## — Asymmetry, Non-Reciprocity, and Power —

---

## **Abstract**

This section develops a theory of support distribution.

Support is not evenly distributed, nor does it necessarily return to its source.  
Those who sustain structures are not always sustained in return.

Thus, asymmetry in support constitutes the condition of power.

---

## **0. Introduction — From Invisibility to Distribution**

In previous sections, support was shown to be:

- structurally invisible
    
- politically concealed
    

This section advances a third condition:

👉 **support is unevenly distributed**

---

## **1. Ideal Symmetry**

A symmetric model assumes:

```text
A supports B  
B supports A
```

In this case:

- support circulates
    
- stability is mutual
    
- persistence is shared
    

---

👉 **support forms a closed loop**

---

## **2. Asymmetry of Support**

In reality:

```text
A supports B  
B does not support A
```

Thus:

- support flows in one direction
    
- maintenance is uneven
    
- persistence is not shared
    

---

👉 **support does not return**

---

## **3. Non-Reciprocity**

Support is often:

- non-reciprocal
    
- non-compensated
    
- non-recognized
    

Examples:

- care work
    
- infrastructure labor
    
- domestic labor
    

---

Thus:

**support is given without return.**

---

## **4. Structural Mapping (ΔR–ΔZ)**

We now map support onto HEG-16:

- ΔR → supporting side (labor, care, operational ground)
    
- ΔZ → supported side (structure, institution, representation)
    

---

Thus:

```text
ΔR supports ΔZ  
ΔZ does not support ΔR
```

---

👉 **structure rests on what it does not sustain**

---

## **5. Emergence of Power**

Power emerges from this asymmetry.

**Definition:**

**Power is the asymmetry of support.**

---

Power determines:

- who receives support
    
- who provides support
    
- whether support returns
    

---

## **6. Invisibility and Asymmetry**

Asymmetry is reinforced by invisibility:

- supporting side remains unseen
    
- supported side appears as self-standing
    

---

Thus:

**what depends appears independent.**

---

## **7. The Weight of Support**

Support becomes visible as weight.

Not as structure,  
but as burden.

---

When asymmetry intensifies:

- effort accumulates
    
- strain increases
    
- imbalance becomes bodily
    

---

Thus:

**support appears as weight on the supporting side.**

---

## **8. Irreversibility**

Non-reciprocal support:

- accumulates without return
    
- cannot be closed
    
- generates structural imbalance
    

---

Thus:

**support is non-closed.**

---

## **9. Repositioning Society**

Society is not a neutral system.

It is:

👉 **a field of asymmetric support relations**

---

## **Conclusion**

Support is uneven, non-reciprocal, and non-closed.

Those who sustain are not always sustained.

Thus:

👉 **power is the asymmetry of support**

---

# **HEG-19｜支えの分配**

## — 非対称・非還元・権力 —

---

## **概要**

本節は支えの分配を論じる。

支えは均等ではなく、循環せず、偏在する。  
支える者が必ずしも支えられるとは限らない。

この非対称性が、権力の条件となる。

---

## **0. 導入 — 不可視から分配へ**

支えは：

- 見えない
    
- 見えなくされる
    

そして：

👉 **偏っている**

---

## **1. 対称モデル**

理想的には：

- 支えは循環する
    

---

## **2. 非対称構造**

現実には：

👉 支えは一方向に流れる

---

## **3. 非還元性**

支えは：

- 戻らない
    
- 補償されない
    
- 認識されない
    

---

## **4. ΔR–ΔZ構造**

- ΔR → 支える側
    
- ΔZ → 支えられる側
    

---

👉 **ΔRはΔZを支えるが  
ΔZはΔRを支えない**

---

## **5. 権力の定義**

**権力とは、支えの非対称である**

---

## **6. 不可視との接続**

- 支える側は見えない
    
- 支えられる側は自立して見える
    

---

## **7. 重さの出現**

支えは構造としては見えない。

しかし：

👉 **重さとして現れる**

---

- 疲労
    
- 負担
    
- 消耗
    

---

👉 身体に現れる

---

## **8. 非閉包**

支えは：

👉 循環しない

---

## **9. 再定義**

社会とは：

👉 **支えの非対称な分配構造である**

---

## ■ 結語

**支えていた者は  
支えられていなかった

そのとき

はじめて

重さが現れた**

---

## ■ 響詠

見えなかったのは

構造ではなく

重さだった
