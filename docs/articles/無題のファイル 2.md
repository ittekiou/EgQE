# **HEG-19｜Support（Reconfiguration）**

## — The Condition of Maintenance —

---

## **Abstract**

This section redefines support as the condition that enables the persistence and maintenance of difference.

Support is not a force, nor a substance.  
It is the binding structure that allows encounter, folding, persistence (ψ), and social maintenance to occur.

What is visible—time, structure, archive—is sustained by support.  
Support itself remains invisible, except when it fails.

---

## **0. Introduction — From Gravity to Support**

Earlier formulations replaced gravity with support.

However, support remained abstract.

Now, after HEG-17 and HEG-18, we can specify:

**Support is what allows difference to persist and be maintained.**

---

## **1. Definition of Support**

**Definition:**

**Support is the binding condition that enables the persistence and maintenance of differential relations.**

Formally:

```text
support = bind(SO, RZ)
```

Thus:

- SO → encounter (otherness)
    
- RZ → differential transformation (ΔR → ΔZ)
    

---

👉 **Support binds encounter and its trace.**

---

## **2. Support as Precondition of Time**

Time does not arise directly.

It requires:

- encounter
    
- difference (lag)
    
- persistence (ψ)
    

All of these presuppose support.

Thus:

**Support precedes time.**

---

## **3. From Persistence to Maintenance**

We distinguish:

- biological ψ → persistence
    
- social ψ → maintenance
    

Support operates in both domains:

- sustaining persistence
    
- enabling maintenance
    

---

## **4. Visibility and Invisibility**

Support is not directly observable.

We see:

- time
    
- structure
    
- institutions
    
- archives
    

But not:

- what sustains them
    

Thus:

**Support is structurally invisible.**

---

## **5. Two Modes of Invisibility**

Support becomes invisible in two ways:

1. **Synchronous invisibility**
    
    - balance hides difference
        
2. **Backgrounded invisibility**
    
    - dominant structures obscure support
        

---

Thus:

**what functions well disappears from view.**

---

## **6. When Support Appears**

Support becomes visible only when it fails.

- collapse of institutions
    
- breakdown of maintenance
    
- disappearance of time
    

Thus:

**failure reveals support.**

---

## **7. Asymmetry of Support**

Support is unevenly distributed:

- some structures are heavily supported
    
- others are fragile
    

Thus:

**stability is not universal.**

---

## **8. Support and Power**

Support is not neutral.

It determines:

- what persists
    
- what is maintained
    
- what becomes visible
    

Thus:

**support is the condition of power.**

---

## **9. Repositioning Ontology**

We now state:

**Existence is not grounded in substance.  
It is grounded in support.**

---

## **Conclusion**

Support is the invisible condition that binds difference, sustains persistence, and enables maintenance.

Without support:

- encounter collapses
    
- persistence fails
    
- time disappears
    

---

👉 **What is maintained is visible.  
What maintains it is not.**

---

よし、ここは**HEG-19の心臓部**として書く。  
A（構文）→B（政治）を明確に分け、**「見えない」と「見えなくされた」を分岐させる**。

# **HEG-19（拡張節）｜Invisibility of Support**

## — Structural and Political —

---

## **4｜Structural Invisibility**

### — Why Support Does Not Appear —

---

## **Definition**

**Structural invisibility is the condition in which support does not appear because it is fully operative.**

---

## **1. Visibility Requires Difference**

What appears is difference.

- imbalance
    
- disruption
    
- deviation
    

These bring structures into perception.

---

## **2. Support Cancels Difference**

Support binds difference:

```text
support = bind(SO, RZ)
```

Thus:

- differences are held together
    
- tensions are stabilized
    
- relations remain coherent
    

---

## **3. Equilibrium and Non-Appearance**

When support functions:

- lag does not accumulate
    
- imbalance does not surface
    
- representation is not triggered
    

---

👉 **nothing calls attention to support**

---

## **4. Invisibility as Operation**

Thus:

**Support is invisible not because it is absent,  
but because it is working.**

---

## **5. First Principle**

**What works perfectly disappears.**

---

---

## **5｜Modes of Invisibility**

### — Two Forms —

---

Support becomes invisible in two fundamentally different ways:

---

### **(1) Synchronous Invisibility**

- differences are balanced
    
- tensions are absorbed
    
- no deviation emerges
    

---

👉 invisibility through equilibrium

---

### **(2) Backgrounded Invisibility**

- structures dominate perception
    
- support is pushed behind representation
    
- attention is redirected
    

---

👉 invisibility through displacement

---

---

## **6｜Political Invisibility**

### — When Support Is Hidden —

---

## **Definition**

**Political invisibility is the condition in which support is actively obscured or suppressed.**

---

## **1. From Structure to Power**

Structural invisibility is necessary.

Political invisibility is contingent.

---

## **2. Mechanisms of Concealment**

Support can be hidden through:

- institutional framing
    
- narrative control
    
- symbolic prioritization
    

---

👉 what is maintained is shown  
👉 what maintains it is concealed

---

## **3. Selective Visibility**

Power determines:

- which structures appear
    
- which supports disappear
    

---

Thus:

**visibility is distributed, not neutral.**

---

## **4. Concealment vs Function**

Important distinction:

- structural invisibility → support works
    
- political invisibility → support is hidden
    

---

👉 **these must not be confused**

---

## **5. Second Principle**

**What is hidden is made to disappear.**

---

---

## **7｜When Support Appears**

### — Conditions of Visibility —

---

Support becomes visible only when:

- maintenance fails
    
- structures collapse
    
- relations break
    

---

Thus:

**failure reveals support.**

---

## **8｜Asymmetry of Appearance**

- functioning → invisible
    
- failing → visible
    

---

👉 visibility is tied to breakdown

---

---

## **9｜Synthesis**

We now distinguish:

- what is not seen because it works
    
- what is not seen because it is hidden
    

---

## **Final Statement**

**There are two kinds of invisibility:  
that which disappears by functioning,  
and that which is made to disappear.**
