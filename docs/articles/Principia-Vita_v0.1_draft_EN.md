# Abstract

Life has traditionally been studied through evolution, metabolism, or information.  
However, these approaches presuppose the existence of life itself.

This paper proposes a natural philosophical framework for the **conditions of life** rather than the mechanisms of its evolution.

Within the Cosmogonica–Physica–Vita trilogy, life is not treated as an accidental product of matter but as a structural phenomenon emerging at the junction of **cosmos and matter**.

Life is defined as a **locally non-closed structure that recursively circulates generation and preservation while maintaining phase transition through the introduction of difference.**

This framework relocates Darwinian evolution within a broader natural philosophy of life.

---

# Prologue

## Life at the Junction of Cosmos and Matter

This work constitutes the third part of the trilogy:

```
Principia Cosmogonica
Principia Physica
Principia Vita
```

Cosmogonica describes the relational emergence of the universe.  
Physica describes the structural persistence of matter.

Life appears **at their intersection**.

Life is therefore not appended to cosmology after the fact.  
It emerges where **relation and support meet encounter**.

---

# I｜Life Is Not Given

Life must not be assumed as a primitive.

Two distinct questions must be separated:

```
conditions of life
≠
evolution of life
```

Darwin addressed the second.

This work addresses the first.

Vita therefore investigates **the structural conditions under which life becomes possible.**

---

# II｜Ground and Protected Liquid

Life requires a specific environmental condition.

```
support
↓
ground
↓
protected liquid
```

Life emerges not merely in liquid environments but in **protected liquid systems sustained by structural support**.

This environment stabilizes the persistence of encounters necessary for life.

---

# III｜Encounter Theory of Life

The fundamental structure of life is encounter.

```
encounter
↓
relation
↓
persistence
↓
life
```

Life is not defined by metabolism alone, nor by reproduction alone.

Life is the **persistence of encounter**.

**Proposition**

```
Life is persistent encounter.
```

---

# IV｜Vital Persistence

Life persists through the dynamic relation between generation and preservation.

```
generation
↔
preservation
```

Pure generation disperses.  
Pure preservation freezes.

Life circulates between the two.

**Proposition**

```
Life is preservation that refuses to freeze.
```

---

# V｜Phase Maintenance

Natural systems tend toward either diffusion or fixation.

```
diffusion
freeze
```

Life exists between these states.

```
diffusion ← life → freeze
```

Life maintains **phase transition conditions** rather than stabilizing a final state.

---

# VI｜Eating

Life requires the continuous introduction of difference.

Without difference, persistence collapses.

Eating is therefore not merely energy intake but **difference introduction**.

```
difference introduction
↓
phase maintenance
↓
life
```

**Proposition**

```
Eating is the introduction of difference that maintains phase transition.
```

---

# VII｜Biosphere

Life does not exist in isolation.

Each living system forms encounter relations with others.

```
life
→ encounter network
→ biosphere
```

The biosphere is the **planetary network of persistent encounters**.

**Proposition**

```
Life calls life.
```

---

# VIII｜Darwin in Range

Darwinian evolution operates **after life has already emerged**.

```
life
↓
variation
↓
selection
↓
evolution
```

Darwin therefore describes the dynamics of life **within the biosphere**, not the origin of life itself.

Vita places evolution **within range** of a broader natural philosophy.

---

# IX｜Life as Non-Closure

Life cannot be defined as a closed system.

Life is instead a **locally non-closed structure** that recursively circulates generation and preservation.

```
generation
↔
preservation
```

When this circulation ceases, life disappears.

**Final Definition**

```
Life is a locally non-closed structure that recursively circulates generation and preservation.
```

---

# X｜Planetary Condition of Life

Life does not emerge everywhere in the universe.

Life emerges under **planetary conditions**.

The biosphere is not merely a collection of organisms but a planetary-scale structure.

Life therefore depends on a specific configuration of matter and environment.

This configuration may be summarized as:

```
cosmos
↓
matter
↓
planet
↓
life
```

Planets provide the conditions in which liquid environments can persist and encounters can stabilize.

These conditions include:

- gravitational support
    
- protected liquid environments
    
- stable energy gradients
    

Within such conditions, encounters become persistent.

Life is therefore not simply a biochemical phenomenon but a **planetary phenomenon**.

Life belongs not only to biology but also to **planetary physics**.

The biosphere must therefore be understood as a **planetary layer of persistent encounters**.

**Proposition**

```
Life is a planetary phenomenon sustained by persistent encounters.
```

---

# Final Definition of Life

We may now state the final definition.

Life is not a substance.

Life is not a molecule.

Life is not an organism.

Life is:

```
a locally non-closed structure
that recursively circulates
generation and preservation
within planetary encounter networks.
```

---

# Vita within the Trilogy

The trilogy now forms a complete structure.

```
Principia Cosmogonica
relation

Principia Physica
support

Principia Vita
encounter
```

From relation emerges matter.  
From support emerges ground.  
From encounter emerges life.

# Natural Philosophy of Life

The aim of _Principia Vita_ is not to replace biology.

Its aim is to provide a **natural philosophical framework** within which biology becomes intelligible.

Biology describes the diversity of life.

Vita describes the **conditions of life itself**.

---

# Closing Proposition

Life is not a rare accident in the universe.

Life is what appears when matter, ground, and encounter persist together.

```
Life is persistent encounter on supported ground.
```
